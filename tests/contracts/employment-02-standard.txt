EMPLOYMENT AGREEMENT

This Employment Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of March 1, 2026, by and between
Clearpath Software Pvt. Ltd., a company incorporated under the Companies Act, 2013, with
registered office at Pune, Maharashtra, India ("Employer"), and Ananya Reddy, residing in
Pune, Maharashtra, India ("Employee").

1. POSITION AND DUTIES

1.1 Employee is appointed as Product Designer and shall report to the Head of Design.

1.2 Employee's duties and responsibilities are described in the attached Job Description
(Exhibit A). Changes to the role or responsibilities shall be discussed with Employee and
documented in writing.

2. COMPENSATION AND BENEFITS

2.1 Employee shall receive an annual gross salary of INR 18,00,000 (Rupees Eighteen Lakhs),
payable monthly on the last working day of each month.

2.2 Employee is eligible for an annual performance bonus of up to 15% of annual gross salary,
based on individual and company performance as evaluated during the annual review process.

2.3 Benefits include: health insurance coverage for Employee and immediate family, 24 days
of paid leave per annum (in addition to public holidays), and an annual learning and
development budget of INR 50,000.

3. PROBATION

3.1 Employee shall be on probation for a period of six (6) months from the date of joining.

3.2 During probation, either party may terminate employment with fifteen (15) days written
notice.

3.3 Upon satisfactory completion of the probation period, Employee shall be confirmed in
writing. Performance evaluation criteria for confirmation are described in Exhibit B.

4. NOTICE PERIOD

4.1 After confirmation, either party may terminate employment by providing thirty (30) days
written notice.

4.2 Either party may provide payment in lieu of notice for the unexpired portion of the
notice period, calculated on the basis of gross monthly salary.

5. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

5.1 Employer owns all intellectual property created by Employee in the course of employment,
during working hours, and using Employer resources, as provided under the Copyright Act, 1957
Section 17(c).

5.2 Employee retains ownership of intellectual property created entirely on personal time,
using personal resources, and unrelated to Employer's business or Employee's role. Employee
shall notify Employer of any such personal projects in writing to avoid ambiguity.

5.3 Employee shall not incorporate any personal intellectual property or third-party
intellectual property into Employer's work without prior written disclosure and approval.

6. NON-COMPETE AND NON-SOLICITATION

6.1 During the term of employment, Employee shall not perform substantially similar design
services for the following named competitors: DesignWorks India Pvt. Ltd. and PixelCraft
Technologies Pvt. Ltd.

6.2 For clarity, no restriction on Employee's right to work, compete, or seek employment
in any capacity shall apply after the termination or expiration of employment. Employee's
post-employment confidentiality obligations under Section 8 survive, but competition
restrictions do not.

7. LEAVE POLICY

Employee is entitled to leave as per the Employer's Leave Policy (available on the internal
HR portal), which includes earned leave, sick leave, and casual leave in accordance with
applicable labor laws.

8. CONFIDENTIALITY

8.1 Employee shall maintain the confidentiality of all non-public business information,
trade secrets, and proprietary data of the Employer during and after employment.

8.2 Confidentiality obligations do not apply to information that becomes publicly available
through no fault of Employee, is independently known by Employee prior to employment, or is
required to be disclosed by law or court order.

8.3 Confidentiality obligations shall survive termination of employment for a period of two
(2) years.

9. CODE OF CONDUCT

Employee shall comply with the Employer's Code of Conduct and workplace policies as
communicated from time to time. The Employer shall provide reasonable notice of material
changes to policies.

10. GOVERNING LAW AND DISPUTE RESOLUTION

10.1 This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of India.

10.2 Any dispute arising out of or in connection with this Agreement shall be resolved by
arbitration under the rules of the Pune International Arbitration Centre. The seat of
arbitration shall be Pune, India.

11. GENERAL PROVISIONS

11.1 This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the parties. Amendments require
the written agreement of both parties.

11.2 Neither party may assign this Agreement without the prior written consent of the other
party, except in connection with a merger or acquisition.
