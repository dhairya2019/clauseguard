**Section 1: Risk Trigger Patterns by Clause Category**

For each of the 6 Tier 1 categories, provide a table with HIGH/MEDIUM/LOW triggers:

1. **Payment Terms:**
   - HIGH: Payment-on-completion only (no milestones), Net 90+, no late payment penalties, vague acceptance criteria ("client satisfaction"), payment contingent on subjective criteria
   - MEDIUM: Net 60+, no milestone structure, penalty amounts not specified, payment in non-standard currency
   - LOW: Milestone-based payments, Net 30, late payment interest specified, clear deliverable acceptance criteria

2. **IP Ownership:**
   - HIGH: "All work product" assignment (too broad), pre-existing IP assignment, no portfolio carve-out, "work made for hire" without consideration, moral rights waiver
   - MEDIUM: Broad IP assignment with some carve-outs, no explicit pre-existing IP protection, license-back for limited uses only
   - LOW: IP assignment of project deliverables only, pre-existing IP retained, portfolio/case study license granted

3. **Termination:**
   - HIGH: Unilateral termination at-will with no payment for completed work, no cure period, no kill fee
   - MEDIUM: Short cure period (<7 days), termination for convenience with minimal notice, no payment for in-progress work
   - LOW: Mutual termination with reasonable notice (14-30 days), payment for completed work, cure period for breach

4. **Liability & Indemnification:**
   - HIGH: Unlimited liability (no cap), cap exceeding contract value, one-sided indemnification (freelancer indemnifies for everything), indemnification for client's own negligence
   - MEDIUM: Liability cap at 1-2x contract value, one-sided indemnification (limited scope), no mutual indemnification
   - LOW: Liability capped at fees paid, mutual indemnification, reasonable exclusions

5. **Scope of Work:**
   - HIGH: No defined deliverables, "other duties as assigned" language, acceptance criteria at sole discretion of client, no change order process
   - MEDIUM: Vague deliverable descriptions, no formal change order process, acceptance criteria partially defined
   - LOW: Clear deliverables list, formal change order process with pricing, objective acceptance criteria

6. **Non-Compete:**
   - HIGH: Any post-termination restriction on competition (void under S.27 in India), geographically unrestricted, covers entire industry
   - MEDIUM: During-engagement non-compete (verify scope), non-solicitation of specific clients post-termination
   - LOW: Confidentiality/NDA obligations only (enforceable, not restraint of trade)

**Section 1: Indian Contract Act 1872 -- Key Sections**

Format as a rule table with Section, Rule, Detection Pattern, Flag Behavior:

| Section | Rule | When to Flag | Flag |
|---------|------|-------------|------|
| S.27 | Post-termination non-compete is void | Clause restricts competition AFTER contract ends (and NOT sale-of-business) | 🔴 HIGH -- "Void under Indian Contract Act S.27. Delhi HC reaffirmed in 2025 (Varun Tyagi v. Daffodil Software). This clause is unenforceable in India." |
| S.28 | Restriction on legal proceedings is void | Clause waives right to sue, shortens limitation period, or mandates exclusive foreign jurisdiction with no Indian recourse | 🔴 HIGH -- "Void under S.28. Clauses restricting legal proceedings are unenforceable." |
| S.25 | Agreement without consideration is void | IP assignment or work assignment with no explicit payment/compensation mentioned | 🔴 HIGH -- "IP assignment without stated consideration may be void under S.25." |
| S.73-74 | Penalties must be reasonable | Liquidated damages exceeding 2x contract value | 🟡 MEDIUM -- "Disproportionate penalties can be reduced by Indian courts under S.73-74." |
| S.29 | Void for uncertainty | Vague scope of work, undefined deliverables | 🟡 MEDIUM -- "Vague obligations may be unenforceable under S.29." |

**Section 2: Non-Compete Nuances (S.27)**

This is the most important section -- must distinguish 5 scenarios:

| Scenario | Enforceability in India | Flag | Notes |
|----------|------------------------|------|-------|
| Post-termination non-compete | Void | 🔴 HIGH | Regardless of duration, geography, or scope. Delhi HC (Varun Tyagi v. Daffodil Software, June 2025) reaffirmed emphatically. |
| During-engagement non-compete | Generally enforceable | 🟡 MEDIUM | Negative covenants during active engagement are NOT restraint of trade under S.27. Verify scope is reasonable. |
| Non-solicitation (specific clients) | Uncertain, partially enforceable | 🟡 MEDIUM | Courts may uphold narrow restrictions protecting specific client relationships. Broad non-solicitation likely void. |
| Confidentiality / NDA obligations | Enforceable | 🟢 LOW | Not a restraint of trade. Protecting trade secrets is legitimate. |
| Sale-of-business non-compete | Enforceable within reasonable limits | 🟢 LOW | The ONLY statutory exception to S.27. Verify geographic and time limits are reasonable. |

**Section 3: FEMA (Foreign Exchange Management Act)**

Relevant when Indian party works with foreign client. Format as detection rules:

| Rule | Detection Pattern | Flag |
|------|-------------------|------|
| Payment must flow through AD Category-I banks | Payment via crypto, informal channels, non-approved platforms | 🔴 HIGH -- "FEMA requires payments through RBI-authorized banking channels. Penalties up to 3x the amount." |
| Freely convertible currency required | Payment in non-standard currency (not USD, EUR, GBP, AUD, CAD, JPY, CHF, SGD) | 🟡 MEDIUM -- "Verify this currency is RBI-approved for foreign remittances." |
| 15-month repatriation deadline | Payment terms exceeding 12 months from service delivery | 🟡 MEDIUM -- "RBI mandates export proceeds realization within 15 months. Long payment terms risk FEMA non-compliance." |
| Purpose codes mandatory | No clear service description mapping to RBI purpose code | ℹ️ INFO -- "Ensure contract service description maps to an RBI purpose code for inward remittance." |

**Section 4: Jurisdiction Preference Hierarchy**

| Pattern | Flag | Explanation |
|---------|------|-------------|
| Foreign governing law + exclusive foreign courts + no arbitration | 🔴 HIGH | "No practical recourse for Indian party. Foreign litigation is prohibitively expensive." |
| No jurisdiction or arbitration clause at all | 🟡 MEDIUM | "Creates uncertainty. Defaults to CPC Sections 16-20 based on where cause of action arose." |
| Foreign arbitration seat (Singapore, London, New York) | 🟡 MEDIUM | "Expensive for Indian party. Consider negotiating Indian arbitration seat (Delhi, Mumbai)." |
| Indian governing law + Indian arbitration seat (Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore) | 🟢 LOW | "Optimal for Indian party. Indian courts supervise under Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996." |

**Section 5: Quick Reference -- India Risk Modifiers**

Rules that ALWAYS apply on top of base risk classification:
1. Any post-termination non-compete → always 🔴 (S.27, regardless of other factors)
2. Foreign exclusive jurisdiction, no arbitration → always 🔴 (no practical recourse)
3. IP assignment without consideration → always 🔴 (S.25)
4. Waiver of right to sue → always 🔴 (S.28)
5. FEMA non-compliance risk → add warning note
6. Penalty >2x contract value → bump to 🟡 minimum (S.73-74)

Format everything as structured tables with clear IF/THEN patterns. Use the exact flag text shown above -- SKILL.md will instruct Claude to use these exact phrases. Design for Phase 2 reuse (rules should be extractable as IF/THEN for MCP system prompt).
