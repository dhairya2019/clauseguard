**Frontmatter (SKILL-01):**
```yaml
---
name: clauseguard
description: >-
  Analyzes legal contracts and clauses for risks, unfavorable terms,
  missing protections, and obligations. Flags India-specific legal issues
  including Section 27 non-compete, FEMA compliance, and jurisdiction risks.
  Use when reviewing contracts, NDAs, SaaS agreements, freelance agreements,
  terms of service, employment contracts, or when the user asks about
  contract risks, clause analysis, or legal review.
argument-hint: "[file-path-or-paste-text]"
allowed-tools: Read, Grep, Glob
---
```
Key: description must be keyword-rich, third-person, with explicit "Use when..." triggers. Include "contract", "NDA", "clause", "legal review", "risks" as activation keywords.

**Step 1 -- Input Handling:**
Handle three $ARGUMENTS scenarios:
1. File path (e.g., `./contracts/nda.txt`) -- use Read tool to load
2. Pasted text -- use directly
3. Empty -- prompt user to paste contract or provide file path

**Step 2 -- Document Type Identification:**
Classify into: Freelance/Independent Contractor Agreement, Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA), Terms of Service/Terms of Use. This is the FIRST analysis step because all downstream analysis depends on correct classification.

**Step 3 -- Clause-by-Clause Extraction:**
Instruct Claude to identify and categorize clauses into 6 Tier 1 categories:
1. Payment Terms
2. IP Ownership
3. Termination
4. Liability & Indemnification
5. Scope of Work
6. Non-Compete

**Step 4 -- Risk Classification (SKILL-04):**
Three-tier system with emoji indicators:
- 🔴 HIGH: Serious harm potential. One-sided, unusual, or potentially unenforceable. Action: "Get a lawyer. Negotiate hard or walk away."
- 🟡 MEDIUM: Negotiable and somewhat unfavorable. Common but improvable. Action: "Negotiate for better terms."
- 🟢 LOW: Standard, balanced, or favorable. Industry-normal. Action: "Acceptable. No action needed."

Include the classification logic:
1. Identify clause category
2. Check HIGH-risk trigger patterns -> RED
3. Check MEDIUM-risk patterns -> YELLOW
4. Default to LOW/GREEN
5. Apply India-specific modifiers (reference india-law.md)

Overall document risk:
- RED: 2+ HIGH-risk clauses, OR 1 HIGH in payment/liability/IP
- YELLOW: 1 HIGH (non-critical) OR 3+ MEDIUM
- GREEN: No HIGH, fewer than 3 MEDIUM

**Step 5 -- India-Specific Rule Checks:**
Instruct Claude to consult `india-law.md` for:
- S.27 non-compete (post-termination = void)
- S.28 restriction on legal proceedings
- S.25 consideration requirements
- FEMA payment channel/currency/timeline rules
- Jurisdiction preference hierarchy
Reference: "For India-specific legal rules and case law, see [india-law.md](india-law.md)"

**Step 6 -- Missing Clause Detection:**
Check against per-document-type checklists. Reference: "For standard clause checklists per document type, see [clause-patterns.md](clause-patterns.md)"

**Step 7 -- Overall Risk Assessment:**
Aggregate clause-level findings into overall document risk level with one-sentence summary.

**Step 8 -- Output Assembly:**
Use the structured markdown output format (see Task 2).

Keep total SKILL.md under 500 lines. Use concise, imperative instructions. Write for Haiku-level model compatibility (explicit step numbering, concrete examples, structured templates).

**Output Format Template (SKILL-02):**
```markdown
## Contract Summary
- Type: [identified type]
- Parties: [party A, party B]
- Key dates: [effective, termination, renewal]

## Risk Overview
| Risk Level | Count |
|------------|-------|
| 🔴 HIGH    | N     |
| 🟡 MEDIUM  | N     |
| 🟢 LOW     | N     |
Overall: [🔴/🟡/🟢] -- [one-sentence summary]

## Clause Analysis

### [Clause N]: [Title]
- **Risk:** [🔴/🟡/🟢] [HIGH/MEDIUM/LOW]
- **What it says:** [plain English summary]
- **Why it matters:** [explanation of impact]
- **India note:** [if applicable -- S.27, FEMA, jurisdiction]
- **Safer alternative:** [suggested rewording framed as "standard market language"]

## Missing Protections
- [Missing clause] -- [why it matters]

## Recommended Actions
1. [Prioritized action items]
```

**Framing Rules (critical for legal compliance):**
DO say: "This clause means...", "Under Indian law, this type of clause is typically...", "Courts have generally held that...", "Consider discussing this clause with a lawyer.", "Standard market language would be..."
DO NOT say: "You should not sign this.", "This clause is illegal." (use "void under S.27" instead), "I recommend...", "You need to...", "Your legal position is..."

**Legal Disclaimer (SKILL-06):**
Add as mandatory footer on every output:
```
---
> **Disclaimer:** This analysis is for informational purposes only and does not
> constitute legal advice. ClauseGuard is not a law firm. For important
> contracts or high-value agreements, consult a qualified advocate
> enrolled with the Bar Council of India.
```

**Escalation Flags (SKILL-07):**
For HIGH-severity issues, add inline escalation callout BEFORE the clause analysis:
```
> **⚠️ Consult a lawyer:** This clause involves [jurisdiction-specific
> enforceability / significant financial exposure / regulatory compliance].
> A qualified advocate can advise on your specific situation.
```

Escalation triggers:
- Any RED clause involving IP assignment of significant value
- Jurisdiction issues affecting enforceability of entire contract
- FEMA compliance concerns with penalty risk
- Penalty clauses with disproportionate amounts (>2x contract value)
- Waiver of fundamental legal rights (S.28)

Verify the complete file stays under 500 lines and reads as a coherent, step-by-step prompt that a Haiku-level model could follow without ambiguity.
