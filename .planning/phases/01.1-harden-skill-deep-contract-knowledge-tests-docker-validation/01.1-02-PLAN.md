- *ONGC v. Saw Pipes* (2003) 5 SCC 705 -- SC guidelines for reasonable compensation under S.74

**Add new Section: Copyright Act 1957** (after existing sections, before FEMA):

| Section | Rule | When to Flag | Flag |
- S.17: Author is first owner of copyright (exceptions: employer, commissioner). Case: *IPRS v. Eastern Indian Motion Pictures* (SC). Flag: HIGH for blanket IP grabs that override default first-ownership.
- S.17(c): Employer owns work created under contract of service (employment). Distinguish from independent contractor -- S.17(c) does NOT apply to freelancers. Flag: MEDIUM -- note the distinction.
- S.57: Moral rights (paternity and integrity) are not assignable. Waiver of moral rights in contract is of questionable enforceability in India. Flag: MEDIUM -- "Moral rights waiver may not be enforceable in India under Copyright Act S.57."

Add detection logic block:
```
IF contract assigns copyright:
  IF party is employee (contract of service):
    -> S.17(c) applies, employer likely owns work product
    -> MEDIUM (verify scope is reasonable)
  ELSE IF party is independent contractor:
    -> S.17 default: author owns copyright
    -> Assignment requires explicit written agreement
    -> IF assignment without stated consideration -> HIGH (S.25 + S.17)
  IF contract waives moral rights:
    -> MEDIUM (questionable enforceability under S.57)
```

**Add new Section: Arbitration and Conciliation Act 1996** (after Copyright Act):

| Provision | Rule | When to Flag | Flag |
- S.7: Arbitration agreement must be in writing. Flag: INFO -- note if arbitration clause exists.
- S.11: Appointment of arbitrators. Unilateral appointment by one party is challengeable. Flag: MEDIUM if only one party appoints arbitrator.
- Part I vs Part II: Part I applies when seat is India; Part II for foreign awards under New York Convention. Flag: INFO -- context for jurisdiction analysis.
- S.12(5) read with Schedule VII: Grounds for challenging arbitrator independence. Flag: MEDIUM if arbitrator has relationship to one party.

```
    -> INFO (may default to court litigation)
  IF one party appoints sole arbitrator:
    -> MEDIUM (challengeable under S.12)
```

**Add new Section: Employment & Labor Law** (after Arbitration Act, before GST):

| Provision | Rule | Case Citation | Flag |
- Employment bond / service bond: *Vijaya Bank v. Prashant Narnaware* (SC 2025, INSC 691) -- employment bonds with liquidated damages upheld if reasonable; unreasonable bonds may be struck down. Flag: MEDIUM -- "Employment bond enforceability depends on reasonableness of amount and duration."
- Garden leave: Paid non-compete during notice period. No definitive Indian case law, but may be enforceable if employer pays full salary during restriction period. Flag: MEDIUM -- "Garden leave enforceability in India is uncertain. Paid restriction during notice period has stronger basis than unpaid."
- Probation termination: 3-6 months standard probation. Employer can typically terminate with minimal notice during probation. Flag: INFO -- context for employment contract analysis.
- Restraint during employment vs after: *Niranjan Shankar Golikari v. Century Spinning & Mfg. Co.* (1967) 2 SCR 378 -- SC upheld reasonable during-employment restrictions. Flag: reinforces S.27 nuance (during-engagement OK, post-termination void).

Add detection logic block:
```
IF contract is employment agreement:
  IF contains service/employment bond:
    IF bond amount > 12 months' salary OR duration > 2 years:
      -> HIGH (likely unreasonable, may be struck down)
    ELSE:
      -> MEDIUM (enforceable if reasonable per Vijaya Bank ruling)
  IF contains garden leave clause:
    IF paid during restriction:
      -> MEDIUM (stronger enforceability)
    ELSE:
      -> HIGH (unpaid garden leave is effectively post-termination non-compete -> S.27)
  IF contains probation terms:
    -> INFO (standard; flag if probation > 6 months)
  IF contains non-compete:
    -> Apply S.27 analysis from Section 2 (same rules apply to employment)
```

**Add new Section: GST for Cross-Border Services** (after Employment):

| Rule | Detail | When to Flag | Flag |
- Zero-rated export of services: Must meet 5 conditions: (1) supplier in India, (2) recipient outside India, (3) place of supply outside India, (4) payment in convertible foreign exchange or INR permitted by RBI, (5) supplier and recipient not merely establishments of same person. Flag: INFO -- "Verify all 5 conditions for 0% GST on service exports."
- S.13(8)(b) deletion (Budget 2025-26): Intermediary services no longer forced to pay 18% GST. Indian intermediaries acting for foreign principals can now claim zero-rated export. Flag: INFO -- "Recent change: intermediary services may now qualify for zero-rated export."
- GST registration threshold: INR 20 lakh aggregate turnover (INR 10 lakh for NE states and special category states). Flag: INFO -- "Freelancers below INR 20 lakh threshold may not need GST registration for domestic services, but cross-border services have different rules."
